How to use this dashboard

A guide to navigating and understanding the data

rocket_launch Getting started

Welcome to the MFAH Regional Housing Dashboard

This dashboard is a shared fact base for the Calgary metropolitan region. It brings together Census, CMHC, CREA, and HART data into a single view so municipal planners, councillors, housing organizations, and community partners can understand housing need, affordability gaps, and market conditions across communities.

Start by selecting communities in the left sidebar. All data, charts, and tables update automatically based on your selection.

checklist Selecting communities
compare_arrows Compare mode (2+ communities)

Select multiple communities to see side-by-side comparisons: charts, regional totals, and tables showing how communities differ on key metrics like CHN, income, and vacancy rates.

filter_1 Single view (1 community)

Select one community and switch to Single View for a deep-dive executive summary with severity ratings, narrative analysis, and detailed breakdowns by income category and housing type.

select_check_box Select all / clear

Use Select All for the full regional picture. Use Clear then pick specific communities for sub-regional comparisons (e.g. corridor analysis or peer benchmarking).

explore Navigating the dashboard
tab Section tabs

Use the tab bar at the top or the sidebar navigation to move between sections. The active section is highlighted with a teal underline. Each section also has a "Next" button at the bottom.

unfold_more Collapsible subsections

Each section has collapsible subsections headed by a guiding question. Click the header to expand. They start collapsed so you can scan the questions first, then drill into what matters.

touch_app Community rows in tables

In detailed tables, click a community name to expand or collapse its data rows. This keeps tables manageable when many communities are selected.

download Export brief

Click Export brief in the top bar to generate a 3-page PDF executive summary for your selected communities — including KPIs, comparison tables, affordability analysis, priority populations, and data sources.

info Important notes

Census timing: The 2021 Census was collected during COVID-19, which may affect employment and commuting indicators. Housing condition data remains reliable.

CMHC coverage: Rental market data covers purpose-built rental buildings (3+ units) only. Secondary rental (basement suites, rented condos) is estimated separately.

Suppressed data: Small communities may show "—" where sample sizes are too small for reliable estimates from Statistics Canada.

Projections: The 2031 household projections are trend-based and should be used as directional guidance, not precise forecasts.

Affordability standard: All affordability calculations use the 30% shelter-to-income ratio. Individual circumstances vary by debt, family size, and other factors.

info Affordability Methodology Disclaimer
Rental affordability: Based on the 30% affordability standard — households should spend no more than 30% of gross income on shelter costs including rent and utilities.

Homeownership affordability: Mortgage calculations assume 25-year amortization, 5.5% interest rate, and 20% down payment. Does not include property taxes, insurance, or maintenance costs.

Housing types: Unit type affordability is based on regional average prices — Condo/Apartment ($300-400K), Townhouse ($400-500K), Semi-detached ($450-550K), Single-detached ($550K+).

Actual affordability depends on individual circumstances including debt levels, credit history, and other expenses. Market conditions vary significantly within communities. Data sources include CMHC, Statistics Canada Census, HART (UBC), and CREA. Contact your local housing authority for current assistance programs.

Data dictionary

Shared definitions for regional alignment

Core Housing Need (CHN)
A household is in CHN if: (1) it falls below adequacy, suitability, or affordability standards, AND (2) it would need to spend 30%+ of before-tax income to access housing meeting all three standards.
Affordability Standard
Housing costs less than 30% of before-tax household income. 30-49% = moderately cost-burdened; 50%+ = severely cost-burdened.
Adequacy Standard
Housing does not require major repairs (defective plumbing/electrical, structural repairs).
Suitability Standard
Housing has enough bedrooms for the household according to National Occupancy Standard.
Area Median Household Income (AMHI)
Income where half earn more, half earn less. Baseline for: Very Low (≤20%), Low (21-50%), Moderate (51-80%), Median (81-120%), High (>120%).
Primary Rental Market
Purpose-built rental housing (3+ unit buildings built specifically for rental). Tracked by CMHC.
Secondary Rental Market
Units not purpose-built: rented condos, basement suites, single-family homes.
Vacancy Rate
% of rental units unoccupied. Healthy: 3-5%. Below 3% = tight market with rent pressure.
Subsidized Housing
Rent geared to income or receiving government subsidies (public housing, social housing, rent supplements).
Below-Market Rent
Units affordable to households earning ≤80% of AMHI (30% of income threshold).
Housing Starts
New construction where foundation work has begun. Tracked by CMHC monthly/annually.
HART Methodology
Housing Assessment Resource Tool (UBC). Census-based tool for CHN and affordable shelter costs. Federal standard for HNA.
Priority Populations
Groups facing disproportionate housing need: seniors, Indigenous peoples, persons with disabilities, immigrants, refugees, women-led households, youth.
Co-operative Housing
Housing owned by non-profit co-op where residents are members who collectively manage the property.

warning Data Limitations & Notes What should I keep in mind when reading this data?
Census 2021 Timing
Data reflects conditions in May 2021 and may not represent current conditions. Housing markets have changed significantly since then.
CMHC Coverage
Rental Market Survey covers structures with 3+ units. Small communities may have suppressed data for confidentiality when sample sizes are small.
Secondary Rental Estimates
Calculated as Census renter households minus CMHC primary rental. May include temporary arrangements not typically considered rental.
Suppressed Values
Where data shows "**" or N/A, Statistics Canada or CMHC has suppressed values for confidentiality (typically fewer than 10 cases).
Projection Uncertainty
2031 projections use trend-based models and should be treated as indicative only. Actual outcomes will vary based on policy, economic conditions, and migration.
Affordability Calculations
Max affordable home prices assume 25yr amortization, 5.5% rate, 20% down payment. Actual qualification varies by household circumstances.

science Methodology notes How are the numbers in this dashboard calculated?
Max Affordable Home Price
Calculated using: 25-year amortization, 5.5% interest rate, 20% down payment, 30% of income to shelter costs. Formula: Monthly payment → mortgage principal → divide by 0.8 for total price.
Max Affordable Rent
Calculated as 30% of annual income ÷ 12 months. HART income thresholds determine max shelter cost for each income category.
Secondary Rental Market
Estimated as: Total Census renter households − CMHC primary rental universe. Includes basement suites, rented condos, single-family rentals.
Affordable housing deficit
Number of households paying more than 30% of income on shelter who cannot access housing meeting all three standards at an affordable price point.
